Energy Commodities 2025

Investing in the renewable energy transition and commodity markets.

Thesis Overview

Global energy demand is undergoing a structural shift as renewables gain ground on fossil fuels. Solar and wind power costs have dropped dramatically, making green energy the cheapest source of new electricity in many regions. At the same time, geopolitical uncertainties continue to roil oil and gas markets, leading to heightened price volatility.

Our outlook for 2025 emphasises opportunities in renewable infrastructure, battery storage and carbon trading schemes, while acknowledging that traditional commodities will remain integral to the energy mix. Navigating this transition requires careful analysis of supply dynamics, policy incentives and technological advances.

Projected Global Energy Mix

Key Drivers

  • Rapid decline in the cost of solar and wind power driving adoption across emerging and developed markets.
  • Battery storage breakthroughs enabling greater penetration of intermittent renewables.
  • Carbon pricing and emissions trading schemes incentivise investment in clean energy.

Risks & Considerations

  • Supply chain bottlenecks for key metals like lithium and copper may constrain growth.
  • Geopolitical tensions can spike oil and gas prices, affecting project economics.
  • Policy uncertainty and changing subsidies could slow the pace of renewable deployment.
